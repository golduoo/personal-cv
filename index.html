Project 2: Cross-Modal Retrieval with CLIP + ESE + FAISS (Individual Project) | 2026-02 to 2026-03

Problem: Addressed the trade-off between retrieval accuracy and efficiency in cross-modal retrieval by exploring high-quality matching under low-dimensional embeddings.

Data: Used the Flickr8k dataset and extracted image-text features from frozen CLIP (ViT-B/32) to build an evaluation set for cross-modal retrieval.

Approach: Reproduced the ICLR 2025 ESE (Espresso Sentence Embeddings) mechanisms of Learn-to-Express and Learn-to-Compress; trained a lightweight projection network (~2.1M parameters) on frozen CLIP features; compressed 512-dimensional embeddings to 128 dimensions; and built both FAISS Flat exact indexes and IVF approximate indexes.

Outcome/Impact: Reduced storage and retrieval overhead by 75% after compression while preserving 91% of original average Recall (74.72% vs 81.68%); at the same 128-dimensional setting, improved Text-to-Image R@5 by 33% and Image-to-Text R@1 by 28% versus direct truncation; enabled millisecond-level large-scale image-text retrieval.

Your Contribution: Independently completed paper reproduction, training pipeline implementation, compression-alignment experiment design, and FAISS retrieval system development.

Tech Stack: PyTorch · OpenCLIP · FAISS
